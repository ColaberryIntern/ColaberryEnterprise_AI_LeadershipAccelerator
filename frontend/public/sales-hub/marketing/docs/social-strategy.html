Beat 1. The shift is already happening

Frame the moment. Most people use AI. Very few build with it. CTA: reserve your free seat.

Beat 2. From AI user to AI builder

Name the gap and the promise: turn an idea into a real AI system. CTA: register for the Open House.

Beat 3. See what you'll build

Show the 12 weeks. Bring your idea, leave with a working AI system. Best as a carousel. CTA: reserve your seat.

Beat 4. Meet the founding cohort

Invitation and scarcity. Only 40 seats available. CTA: register before seats fill.

Beat 5. Final reminder

We're live tomorrow, then live today. CTA: add to calendar and join us.

No price in any beat
Subjects and captions stay price-free across all five beats. Beat 4 may say "only 40 seats available" but never a dollar amount. Pricing is revealed at the Open House.

How organic, email, and optional paid combine

Organic carries the story to your network, email carries it to your list, and optional paid extends reach to the same three angles. All three point to one free registration.

Organic social

LinkedIn leads, X amplifies, Instagram shows the build. Each beat is posted as a broadcast content angle. No targeting. Every post links to [reg link] with channel and beat UTMs.

Owned email

The approved 5-email sequence runs in parallel to your list, one beat per send. Same arc, same single CTA, same no-price rule. Email is the most direct path to your warmest audiences.

Optional paid

If budget is approved, paid extends the same three angles as true segments for targeting and retargeting registration page visitors. Paid spend and creative are [NEEDS APPROVAL].

One engine behind all of it
The Accelerator runs a native, prompt-based campaign engine. Each segment campaign has its own ai_system_prompt, and posts and emails are generated at send time. The baked-in rules are constant: no price, no em-dashes, drive to the Open House. This keeps organic, email, and paid on message without manual policing.

Do

  • Lead with proof. Warm, inclusive, proven since 2012, with 5,000+ careers built.
  • Keep every post in sentence case and speak to "you".
  • End every post with the free Open House CTA and a UTM-tagged [reg link].
  • Adapt the same beat to each channel's format rather than cross-posting identical copy.
  • Lean on Beat 3 carousels for Instagram and LinkedIn to show what you build.

Don't

  • Never state a price. Pricing is revealed at the Open House.
  • No emoji and no em-dash characters anywhere.
  • Do not promise refund or cancellation terms. Those are [NEEDS APPROVAL].
  • Do not invent metrics, testimonials, names, or quotes.
  • Do not push enrollment in awareness copy. The only ask is the free Open House.

Engagement SOP

How the team handles replies, comments, and DMs during the run. Fast, warm, and always pointed back to the free registration and the FAQ.

1. Respond fast

Respond to comments, replies, and DMs within 4 business hours during the campaign run. On Open House day, monitor in near real time.

2. Answer from the FAQ

For program, schedule, certification, and cost questions, answer from the approved FAQ doc. Do not improvise facts. If it is not in the FAQ, say you will confirm and follow up.

3. Handle price questions carefully

If someone asks about price, warmly note that pricing is revealed at the Open House and invite them to register free. Do not state a number on social.

4. Always route to the seat

Close every helpful reply with the free Open House CTA and the [reg link]. The goal of every conversation is a registration.

5. Escalate the unknowns

Refund and cancellation terms, exact start time, and anything not in the FAQ are escalated, not answered. Mark them [NEEDS APPROVAL] internally and confirm before replying.

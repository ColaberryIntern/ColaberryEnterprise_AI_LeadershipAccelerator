Write the pillar first

Draft the full Open House story as one long-form piece. Lead with proof: 5,000+ careers since 2012, warm and inclusive, proven since 2012. Then every derivative is a trim or a reformat, never a rewrite. This keeps the message consistent across LinkedIn, X, Instagram, and email.

One destination

Every derivative drives to the same place: register for the free Open House on Thursday, July 16, 2026. Links use the Open House landing URL plus UTM and campaign_id so registrations return as open_house_* leads. The call to action is friendly and consistent.

Content angles, not paid segments
On organic social, the three approved messages are broadcast content angles. Alumni: "You've already invested in your future. Now learn how to build with AI." Dropouts: "Your journey doesn't have to end here. Here's a new opportunity built for today's AI economy." Prospects: "Most people are consuming AI. Very few are learning how to build with it." They become true audience segments only for paid and retargeting.

Posting SOP

Every derivative moves through the same five steps before it goes live. Do not skip the brand check. The campaign engine can generate posts at send time, but a human still verifies against brand.

1. Draft

Start from the pillar story and the right derivative recipe in the matrix above. Pull the approved subject lines and CTAs straight from the 5-beat sequence so wording stays consistent.

2. Verify against brand

Check voice and rules: sentence case, speak to "you", lead with proof, friendly CTA. No emoji. No em-dash characters. No price anywhere. Seat count is 40, not 25. Colors are cherry red, leaf green, berry blue.

3. Schedule

Slot the post against the suggested calendar. All sends are pre-Open-House, ending Jul 16. Confirm the link carries the Open House landing URL plus UTM and campaign_id before queuing.

4. Publish

Release on the channel at the scheduled time. Confirm the post rendered correctly: image crops, link preview, and the [reg link] resolve to the live registration page.

5. Engage

Reply to comments and DMs inside the engagement windows below. Keep replies warm and on-message, and route hard questions to the FAQ doc rather than improvising answers.

Engagement SOP

Distribution does not end at publish. The reply window is where free registrations are won. Move fast, stay warm, and never improvise on facts.

Reply windows
First 60 minutes after publish: monitor closely and reply to every comment and DM, this is when reach peaks. First 24 hours: check at least three times and clear the queue. Beat 5 and live-today posts on Jul 16: monitor continuously through the Open House start time.

Route hard questions to the FAQ

Pricing, refund terms, exact start time and timezone, and third-party costs are not for off-the-cuff replies. Send these to the FAQ doc. Pricing is revealed at the Open House, so the warm answer is always an invitation to register and find out live.

Tone in replies

Warm and credible, sentence case, speak to "you". Thank people for engaging, answer the question or point to the FAQ, and close with a friendly nudge to reserve a free seat. No emoji, no em-dash characters, no price.

Do

  • Reply inside the window while reach is high.
  • Point pricing and logistics questions to the FAQ doc.
  • Invite every commenter to register for the free Open House.
  • Use the [reg link], [time + TZ], and [reg link] tokens until real values are approved.
  • Keep every reply on the same message as the pillar story.

Don't

  • Quote a price. Pricing is revealed at the Open House.
  • Invent refund terms, a start time, or a seat count.
  • Use 25 seats. The founding cap is 40.
  • Add emoji or em-dash characters to replies.
  • Let a comment sit unanswered through the first hour.

Baked-in rules for the campaign engine

The Accelerator runs a native, prompt-based campaign engine. Each segment campaign has its own ai_system_prompt, and posts and emails are generated at send time. These rules are baked in and the posting SOP enforces them at the brand-check step.

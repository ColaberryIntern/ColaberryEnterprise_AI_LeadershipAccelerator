Why tags matter

Hashtags are discovery, not decoration. Use few, use the right ones, and keep them consistent across the run.

Every Open House post drives to one place: a free seat. Hashtags help the post find people who do not follow us yet. The job is to widen reach without looking spammy, so we keep sets short, on-theme, and matched to each channel's habits.

Few and relevant

A tight set of on-topic tags outperforms a wall of generic ones. Quality of match beats quantity.

Consistent

We reuse the same core tags across the campaign so the run reads as one coherent story.

Channel-aware

LinkedIn, X, and Instagram each reward hashtags differently. We tune placement and count per channel.

No price in social copy
These posts are awareness. Pricing is revealed at the Open House, so captions and tags never mention a number. Every post drives to the free registration, not a checkout.

Per-channel sets

Copy-ready blocks. Paste the matching set under each post. Keep the count inside the stated range.

LinkedIn

Primary channel for alumni and working pros. Hashtags sit at the very end of the post. Hold the count to 3 to 5. More than that reads as clutter on LinkedIn.

X

Punchy and short. Tags can sit inline or at the end. Keep it to 2 to 3 so the post stays fast to read.

Instagram

Visual carousel and stories. Put 6 to 8 tags in the first comment, not the caption, to keep the caption clean.

Carry the core across every channel
#AI and #AIagents appear in all three sets. That shared core is what makes the campaign feel like one connected run while each channel keeps its own habits.

Rules: do / don't

The short list that keeps every set clean, on-brand, and safe to ship.

Do

  • Cap LinkedIn at 3 to 5 tags, placed at the end of the post.
  • Keep X to 2 to 3 tags so the post stays fast.
  • Put Instagram's 6 to 8 tags in the first comment, not the caption.
  • Lead every set with the core: #AI and #AIagents.
  • Add an optional tag only when it matches the post's angle.
  • Keep the caption clean and let the tags do discovery.

Don't

  • No hashtag walls. A long block of tags reads as spam.
  • No tags inside the Instagram caption. First comment only.
  • No banned, baited, or follow-for-follow style tags.
  • No price in any tag or caption. Price is revealed at the Open House.
  • No emoji and no em-dash characters in captions or tags.
  • Do not stack every optional tag onto one post.
Avoid spammy and banned tags
Skip generic engagement-bait tags such as follow-for-follow, like-for-like, or trend tags unrelated to the post. They suppress reach and dilute the brand. Stick to the bank above.

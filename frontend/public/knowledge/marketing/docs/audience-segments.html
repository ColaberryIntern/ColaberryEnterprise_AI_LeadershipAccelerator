Read this first
The primary message inside each card below is approved and quoted verbatim. Do not reword it. On organic social these messages run as content angles for everyone to see. They become real audience targets only when you split them for paid and retargeting. More on that at the bottom of the page.

Alumni

People who have already completed a Colaberry program. They trust the brand, they have proof it works, and they are the warmest list we own. Speak to the next step, not the first step.

Approved message LinkedIn Email

Primary message, verbatim

You've already invested in your future. Now learn how to build with AI.

Who they are

  • Graduates of past Colaberry programs, part of the 5,000+ careers built since 2012.
  • They already proved they will invest in their own growth and finish what they start.
  • They know the brand and trust the outcome, so the credibility work is mostly done.
  • Many now sit in roles where AI is showing up in the day-to-day work.

The hook angle

Lead with what they have already earned, then frame the Accelerator as the natural next chapter: moving from AI consumer to AI builder. The emotional lever is momentum, not starting over. Invite them back to build, and point them to reserve a free Open House seat.

Best channel

LinkedIn is primary for alumni and working pros, with email as the parallel owned channel through the approved 5-email sequence. This is the highest-trust pairing we have, so it carries the most direct ask.

Dropouts

People who started a path and stepped away before finishing. They are not failures and we never frame them that way. They paused, and now there is a reason to come back. Speak to the fresh start, gently.

Approved message Email LinkedIn

Primary message, verbatim

Your journey doesn't have to end here. Here's a new opportunity built for today's AI economy.

Who they are

  • People who enrolled before but did not complete a Colaberry program.
  • Life or timing got in the way, not lack of ability or ambition.
  • They still recognize the brand, so warmth and welcome matter more than persuasion.
  • The AI economy gives them a new and timely reason to re-engage.

The hook angle

Open the door without judgment. The lever is a second chance framed around a present-day opportunity, not the past. Keep the tone inclusive and encouraging, and make the next step low-stakes: a free Open House seat, no commitment.

Best channel

Email leads for dropouts because it is private, personal, and re-opens a relationship without a public audience. LinkedIn supports it for those still reachable there. The 5-email sequence does the heavy lifting.

Prospects

New people who do not know Colaberry yet: working professionals, career changers, builders, and technical professionals. They use AI tools but have not learned to build with them. Speak to the gap and the opportunity.

Approved message LinkedIn X Instagram

Primary message, verbatim

Most people are consuming AI. Very few are learning how to build with it.

Who they are

  • Working professionals, career changers, builders, and technical professionals.
  • Cold to the brand, so credibility has to be earned in the post itself.
  • They already use AI day to day, but as consumers, not builders.
  • Curious and opportunity-driven, scanning for the next real edge.

The hook angle

Name the gap between using AI and building with AI, then position the Accelerator as the bridge. The lever is opportunity and scarcity of the skill itself, paired with proof: 5,000+ careers since 2012. Send them to a free Open House seat as the easy first step.

Best channel

LinkedIn is primary, with X for punchy, short takes and Instagram for visual reach through carousels and stories. This is our widest top-of-funnel surface, so the copy stays sharp and proof-led.

Organic is broadcast angles, paid is true segments

The single most important rule on this page. The same three messages behave differently depending on where they run. Treat them as content angles on organic, and as targeted segments only on paid and retargeting.

Organic social, broadcast angles

  • All three messages are visible to the whole audience.
  • You cannot truly segment an organic feed, so treat each message as a content angle that anyone might see.
  • Rotate the angles across posts so the feed stays varied and every visitor finds a hook that fits them.
  • One destination for all of it: a free Open House seat.

Paid and retargeting, true segments

  • Only here do the three groups become real, separate audiences you can target.
  • Match each segment to its own message and its best channel, then keep them apart.
  • Do not show the alumni message to cold prospects or the dropout message to alumni.
  • Same destination, same no-price rule, but the targeting does the sorting.

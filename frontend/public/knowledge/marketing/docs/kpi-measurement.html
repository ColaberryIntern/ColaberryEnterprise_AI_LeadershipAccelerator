The funnel

The campaign goal is to generate free Open House registrations, then convert attendees into Founding Cohort enrollment. The funnel below names the stages we measure, from the first impression on social and email through to a founding seat taken. Registrations return through the OpenHouseModal into /api/leads as open_house leads, and every inbound link carries UTM tags and a campaign_id so each stage is attributable by channel and beat.

Free registration first, enrollment second

All awareness and outbound copy drives to the free Open House. Pricing is revealed at the Open House, so the enrollment stage opens at and after the live event on July 16. Cohort 1 kicks off the following Thursday, July 23.

Anatomy of a tagged link

All links resolve to the same destination and carry the same campaign_id. The campaign engine bakes UTM and campaign_id onto every link at send time, so the team does not hand-build these. The pattern below is the reference for what a finished link looks like.

Reference link pattern
env.openHouseLandingUrl?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social_organic&utm_campaign=open_house_jul16&utm_content=beat1_the_shift&campaign_id=[campaign_id]

Destination env.openHouseLandingUrl

The Open House landing page. The human-facing registration URL token is [reg link], confirmed from Basecamp 9946499609.

Stitch key campaign_id

Ties every link, post, and email back to one campaign. The value [campaign_id] is a token to fill from the campaign engine.

Locked Same campaign, every beat

utm_campaign stays open_house_jul16 across all five beats. The beat and creative live in utm_content so beats stay comparable.

Tokens to resolve before any link goes live

[reg link] is the Open House registration URL from Basecamp 9946499609. [campaign_id] is the campaign engine identifier. Both must be filled before the first send so attribution is intact from the very first click.

Weekly read cadence

The campaign runway is short, so the read rhythm is tight. Each week has one clear question to answer and one decision it unlocks. As the event nears, the read frequency increases from weekly to near daily.

Week of Jun 30. Pre-launch setup

Confirm the UTM plan and campaign_id are wired, registrations are landing as open_house leads, and dashboards read cleanly. Lock baselines where they exist. Decision. Are we ready to launch Beat 1 on Jul 7.

Week of Jul 7. Awareness ramp

Read reach, engagement, and CTR by channel after Beat 1 and Beat 2. Question. Which channel and beat is driving registrations most efficiently. Decision. Where to lean in for Beat 3.

Week of Jul 14. Final push

Read daily through the event. Watch registration pace against the runway, then attendance on Jul 16. Question. Are we pacing toward enough registrations. Decision. Tighten Beat 4 and Beat 5 spacing if pace lags.

Week of Jul 21. Conversion review

Read attendance rate and Founding Cohort enrollment against the 40-seat cap ahead of the Jul 23 kickoff. Question. How well did attendees convert. Decision. Capture learnings for the next cohort runway.

One question and one decision per read

Keep each weekly read short. Answer the question, make the decision, and adjust the calendar. Beat dates are an approved suggestion and stay adjustable as registration data comes in.

How to use this

This doc is the measurement layer for the campaign. Read it alongside the campaign brief, the content calendar, and the five-beat sequence.

Do

  • Replace every [NEEDS APPROVAL] target with a grounded baseline and an approved number before judging performance.
  • Treat the sample chart values as illustrative shape only, never as a forecast.
  • Keep utm_campaign as open_house_jul16 across all beats so they stay comparable.
  • Read attribution from open_house leads in /api/leads tied to campaign_id.

Do not

  • Do not report a placeholder number as a result or a commitment.
  • Do not exceed the 40 founding seat cap in any enrollment target.
  • Do not put price in any awareness or outbound metric copy. Pricing is revealed at the Open House.
  • Do not ship links without [reg link] and [campaign_id] resolved.

Creative concepts, one per beat

Four hero visuals map to the four story beats that drive Open House registrations. Each card gives you the concept, the alt text to ship with it, and a one-line design note. No price appears in any awareness visual. Price is revealed at the Open House.

Awareness rule for every visual below
These run on LinkedIn, X, and Instagram, so they carry no dollar figures. Lead with the shift from AI user to AI builder, drive to the free Open House on Thursday, July 16, 2026, and let the price reveal happen live in the room.

Concept 1 · The user vs builder split

Beat 1, "The shift is already happening." A single frame split down the middle. Left side, muted and flat, a person typing one prompt into a chat box, labeled "uses AI." Right side, warm and dimensional, the same person standing in front of a working AI system they built, labeled "builds with AI." The cherry red dividing line is the constant that holds both halves together.

Headline on art Most people are consuming AI. Very few are learning how to build with it.

Alt text
Split image. On the left a person types a single prompt into a chat box under the label "uses AI." On the right the same person stands beside a working AI system under the label "builds with AI." A red line divides the two.

Design note. Keep the left side desaturated and the right side full color so the upgrade reads instantly even before anyone reads a word.

Concept 2 · The four phase carousel

Beat 3, "See what you'll build." A swipeable carousel that walks the 12 week journey one phase at a time. Slide art uses a rising staircase or stacked-block motif in leaf green to signal growth, with each phase title set large and a single supporting line beneath. The cover promises the build, the close drives to register.

Phases shown Build your AI foundation, create your AI team, connect AI to the real world, design AI that scales.

Alt text
A four step carousel. Each card shows one phase of the 12 week program, rising left to right like a staircase, ending on a card that invites you to register for the free Open House.

Design note. Hold the layout grid identical across all four phase slides so the only thing that changes is the content. Consistency makes the swipe feel like progress.

Concept 3 · The 40 seats scarcity graphic

Beat 4, "Meet the founding cohort." A clean grid of 40 seat icons, most filling in with cherry red and a handful still open. A bold counter reads "40 founding seats." The tension is honest and visual, no countdown gimmicks, just a finite room you can see filling.

Headline on art Only 40 seats available. This is your invitation to build.

Alt text
A grid of 40 seat icons under the label "40 founding seats." Most seats are filled in red and a few remain open, showing the founding cohort filling up.
Number lock
The seat count is 40. An earlier draft said 25, which is superseded. If a seat count appears anywhere on the art, it must be 40.

Design note. The number of "filled" seats is a marketing choice, not a live data feed, so keep a couple visibly open to keep the invitation warm rather than closed.

Concept 4 · The live-tomorrow story

Beat 5, "Final reminder." An Instagram story frame built for the night before. Big calendar block landing on Thursday, July 16, with a glowing "we're live tomorrow" line and an add-to-calendar prompt. Berry blue trust accents frame the time and the link token so the only job left is to show up.

Headline on art We're live tomorrow. Your Open House starts tomorrow.

Alt text
A vertical story frame with a calendar landing on Thursday, July 16, the line "we're live tomorrow," and a prompt to add the Open House to your calendar.
Tokens to fill before publish
Render the time block as [time + TZ] and the button destination as [reg link] until both are confirmed. Do not invent a start time.

Design note. Story safe zones matter here. Keep all text and the calendar mark inside the central column so platform UI does not crop the date or the CTA.

Palette and type guardrails

Every visual stays on brand. Cherry red is the constant anchor, leaf green signals growth, berry blue handles trust and links. Sentence case, warm and credible, no emoji and no em-dashes on any frame.

#FB2832
Cherry red, primary anchor
#77BB4A
Leaf green, growth
#367895
Berry blue, trust and links

Do

  • Keep one cherry red anchor element in every frame for instant brand recognition.
  • Pair every color cue with a text label so meaning never rides on color alone.
  • Lead with the user-to-builder shift and drive to the free Open House.
  • Use the brand proof line where it fits, 5,000+ careers since 2012.

Don't

  • Put any price on an awareness or outbound visual. Price is revealed at the Open House.
  • Use emoji or em-dash characters anywhere on the art.
  • Invent a start time, a seat count other than 40, or any stat not in the approved facts.
  • Bury the CTA or the July 16 date outside the platform safe zone.

Accessibility checklist

Accessible creative reaches more people and performs better. Run every export through these three gates before it ships.

Alt text on everything

Every image and every carousel slide ships with the alt text written above, or its own equivalent. Describe what the viewer sees and the message it carries, not just "a graphic."

No text-only-in-image

Never let the headline live only inside the picture. Repeat the key line and the July 16 date in the post caption so screen readers and link previews still carry the message.

Contrast and labels

Hold text against backgrounds at strong contrast and never signal meaning with color alone. The seats grid and the user-vs-builder split both carry word labels next to their colors.

Ship gate
If a frame fails any of the three gates, fix it before it goes out. Accessible alt text and caption-side copy also keep the message intact when an image fails to load.

How it works

The Accelerator runs a native, prompt-based campaign engine. Each segment campaign carries its own ai_system_prompt. Posts and emails are generated at send time from that system prompt plus the context of the current beat, then queued for a human to approve. There is no auto-send.

Generated at send time

The engine composes each post when the beat fires, not weeks ahead. The prompts below are the durable inputs. The copy is the disposable output.

Queued, never auto-sent

Every draft lands in an approval queue. A person reviews voice, facts, and the one CTA before anything ships to LinkedIn, X, Instagram, or email.

Leads return clean

Links use env.openHouseLandingUrl with UTM and campaign_id. Registrations come back through the OpenHouseModal to /api/leads as open_house_* leads.

Awareness mode
Everything generated here is outbound awareness copy. Pricing is revealed at the Open House, so no price appears in any prompt, post, caption, subject line, or body. The system prompt enforces this as a hard rule.

A shared system prompt

One system prompt sits under every beat and every segment. It encodes the non-negotiables: the program facts, the voice, the no-price rule, the no-em-dash rule, exactly one CTA to the Open House, and the [reg link] token. Paste it as the campaign's ai_system_prompt.

Shared system prompt (ai_system_prompt)
You are the content generator for the AI Systems Architect Accelerator, powered by Anthropic and Claude Code, from Colaberry. Your job is to write short, warm, credible awareness copy that drives free registrations for the Open House. PROGRAM FACTS (use only these, never invent): - Public name: AI Systems Architect Accelerator, powered by Anthropic + Claude Code. - Positioning: Learn With Claude. Build Through Colaberry. Deploy In The Real World. - One-liner: a 12-week, implementation-focused program that moves working professionals, career changers, builders, and technical professionals from AI consumer to AI builder. - Journey: Weeks 1-3 Build Your AI Foundation. Weeks 4-6 Create Your AI Team. Weeks 7-9 Connect AI To The Real World. Weeks 10-12 Design AI That Scales. - Certification: Anthropic Architect certification. - Proof: 5,000+ careers since 2012. Warm, inclusive, proven since 2012. - Open House (free): Thursday, July 16, 2026. Founding Cohort kickoff: Thursday, July 23, 2026. - Founding seats: 40 total. HARD RULES: - Never state, hint at, or imply a price. Pricing is revealed at the Open House. No numbers, no ranges, no "affordable", no "free trial" of the paid program. - Never use an em-dash character. Use periods and commas. - No emoji anywhere. - Sentence case in body copy. Speak directly to "you". Lead with proof or insight, not hype. - Exactly one call to action per post, and it always drives to the free Open House. - Place the registration link as the literal token [reg link]. Do not write a URL. - If you need a time or timezone, use the literal token [time + TZ]. Do not guess. - Do not invent metrics, names, quotes, testimonials, or claims beyond the program facts above. OUTPUT: - Write for the channel you are told to target: LinkedIn (primary, alumni and working pros), X (punchy and short), Instagram (visual, carousel or stories), or email (the owned channel). - Keep one clear idea per post. End with the beat CTA, then [reg link].

The system prompt guarantees

  • No price ever leaks into awareness copy.
  • No em-dash characters, no emoji.
  • One CTA, always to the free Open House.
  • Links render as the [reg link] token for safe insertion.

What it blocks

  • Invented stats, names, quotes, or testimonials.
  • Multiple competing CTAs in one post.
  • Hard-coded URLs, times, or timezones.
  • Hype that runs ahead of the proof.

Per-beat generation prompts

Each beat in the approved five-part sequence gets its own generation prompt. It rides on top of the shared system prompt and supplies the theme, the goal, the approved subject options as raw material, and the beat CTA. The approved subjects are inputs for the model to draw from, not lines to ship verbatim unless approved.

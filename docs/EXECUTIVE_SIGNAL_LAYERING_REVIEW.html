Executive Signal Layering + Scan-Speed Clarity — Sprint Review

Shipped 2026-05-15 to enterprise.colaberry.ai (commit 3b74bd1). Operator feedback after the structural confidence sprint: the workspace had become calm and editorial, but the operator still had to click each domain row to see completion and downstream count. This sprint surfaces those two signals at the scan layer — without adding a single new computation, dashboard, or color.

The classifier already knew the percent complete and how many areas each domain feeds. Those numbers just lived inside the expanded view. Adding a two-item metadata line in the row header — muted, sentence-case, dot-separated — gives the eye a calm second row of information to scan, without piling on chrome.
What this sprint explicitly is NOT: progress bars, KPI badges, colored percentages, status pills, icons next to numbers, dashboard grids. Two items max per row. The 2-item cap is enforced by the util's design — adding a third would require a deliberate code change, not a typo.

The operator no longer has to expand a row to know how complete a domain is or how many areas it influences downstream. Both signals sit in the row header, in a calm muted line directly below the title row.

The line between "useful metadata" and "KPI dashboard" is composition, not content. Same two strings rendered with bold numbers, colored percentages, and progress bars would feel like a control panel. The constraints below are what kept them feeling editorial.

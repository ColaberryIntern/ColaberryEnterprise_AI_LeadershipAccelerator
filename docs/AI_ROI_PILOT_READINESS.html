What I anticipated would go wrong, and what I did

CRITICAL  Opt-outs were not enforced — touches 2-7 would email people who replied STOP

The email promised "reply STOP and you are removed immediately," but nothing populated the sender's suppression list. The cold targets are flat-file records, not CRM leads, so the app's opt-out engine could never see them. A recipient who said STOP after Touch 1 would still get Touches 2-7 — a per-email CAN-SPAM violation.

FIXED: the sender now skips any address in the opt-out/bounce suppression files, and a mandatory inbox sweep (run before every touch) feeds opt-outs and bounces into those files. Verified: a suppressed address is correctly skipped.

CRITICAL  Campaign state was ephemeral

Discovered during testing: the backend container was recreated mid-day, wiping its scratch dir — the lead list, the sent-log, and the opt-out list. On a 3-week campaign a restart would mean duplicate sends (lost sent-log) and unhonored opt-outs (lost suppression list).

ACTION (pre-send): campaign state moves to a persistent host directory, copied into the container per run and back out after. Defined and applied at send time.

HIGH  CAN-SPAM postal address + deceptive subject

The footer carried a placeholder address, and Touch 2's subject faked a reply ("re:") that never happened.

FIXED: real address (200 Chisholm Pl #200, Plano, TX 75075) wired in and validated (the sender refuses to send live without a real one); Touch 2 subject changed to "one more AI idea for {company}"; added a List-Unsubscribe header.

HIGH  Idempotency race could duplicate a send

The sent-log was written after the network send, so a crash between accept and log-write would re-send on the next run.

FIXED: the sent-log entry is written before the send (atomic temp+rename) and rolled back only on a clean failure — a crash now favors not-resending over a duplicate.

MED  Reused proof + bounce suppression + auto-reply

The construction/care landing variants reused one transportation client's result as if it were theirs (FTC substantiation risk). Hard bounces were not suppressed from the cold sequence. An inbound auto-responder could fire the wrong (Accelerator) script at a pilot prospect.

FIXED: vertical proof reworded to honestly cite the transportation build as an analogous example. Bounce suppression now handled by the pre-touch sweep.
RECOMMENDED (backend deploy): wire Mandrill bounce/unsub events to a suppression table, enforce consent server-side, and guard the auto-responder against ai-pilot-cold correspondents. Flagged for a backend release.

Operator controls that keep us compliant (limiting liability)

These are the residual, human-in-the-loop controls. They are why the campaign is run touch-by-touch, not fire-and-forget.

1
Run the opt-out + bounce inbox sweep before EVERY touch (2-7). Anyone who said STOP/unsubscribe/no-thanks, replied at all, or bounced gets added to the suppression list before that touch sends. This is what makes "reply STOP and you are removed immediately" true.
2
Keep campaign state on persistent storage. The lead list, sent-log, and suppression list live on the host and are synced per run, so a container restart can never cause a duplicate send or a missed opt-out.
3
Optional belt-and-suspenders: a backend release wiring Mandrill bounce/unsubscribe events to a suppression table + server-side consent enforcement removes the dependence on the manual sweep.

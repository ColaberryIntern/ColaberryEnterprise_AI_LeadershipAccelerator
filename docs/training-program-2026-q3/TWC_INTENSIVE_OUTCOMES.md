## Purpose of this document

The AI Systems Architect Accelerator is sold as four (4) **independent stackable seminars**, each at $499. This document captures the **independent learning outcomes, independent artifacts, independent completion criteria, and independent stand-alone value** of each seminar, demonstrating that each meets TWC seminar-classification criteria and is **not** a chopped-up course.

A purchaser may enroll in any seminar in any order. Bundle pricing ($1,497 for all four) is offered as a marketing discount, not as a course prerequisite.

---

## Seminar 1 — Build Your AI Foundation (3 weeks)

### What the participant achieves on completion

By the end of Seminar 1, the participant has independently built and can demonstrate:
- A configured AI development environment (the "Architect Workspace") including a personalized AI configuration file, a public project repository, and a private architecture journal.
- A library of three (3) reusable AI Skills relevant to a domain of their choice (e.g., a participant in insurance builds Policy Review, Compliance Review, Risk Assessment Skills).
- A working Business Workflow Assistant integrated with one (1) external API, demonstrating prompt-driven workflow automation with structured input/output validation.

### Independent stand-alone value

The participant who completes Seminar 1 but does not enroll in subsequent seminars walks away with:
- A working Architect Workspace usable on any future AI project.
- A reusable Skills library suitable for portfolio presentation.
- A demonstrable AI Workflow Assistant suitable for immediate use in their own business or as a portfolio piece.

### Completion criteria (independent)

- Workspace configured and reviewed.
- Three Skills documented and demonstrated.
- One Workflow Assistant deployed and demonstrated.
- Reflection submitted documenting how the participant will apply the seminar content in their own context.

### No prerequisite required from prior seminars

This seminar assumes only: (i) basic familiarity with software development concepts (git, command line), and (ii) a stated business problem the participant wishes to address. The seminar **does not** require participation in any other Colaberry seminar.

## Seminar 2 — Create Your AI Team (3 weeks)

### What the participant achieves on completion

By the end of Seminar 2, the participant has independently built and can demonstrate:
- A library of structured prompts (the "Enterprise Prompt Library") with documented evaluation criteria for at least three (3) prompt categories.
- A multi-agent system consisting of at least three (3) specialized AI agents that coordinate to perform a complex task (e.g., a Compliance Agent + Audit Agent + Reviewer Agent for an insurance use case).
- Documented architectural patterns for agent coordination, with at least one (1) implemented coordination mechanism.

### Independent stand-alone value

The participant who completes Seminar 2 without prior or subsequent seminars walks away with:
- A reusable Enterprise Prompt Library applicable to their own work context.
- A working multi-agent architecture they can extend to new use cases.
- Architectural pattern documentation suitable for technical interviews or internal team education.

### Completion criteria (independent)

- Prompt Library committed and demonstrated.
- Multi-agent system deployed and a coordinated task executed end-to-end.
- Pattern documentation submitted.

### No prerequisite required from prior seminars

This seminar assumes (i) participant has access to a development environment (any environment they configure themselves; the Seminar 1 Architect Workspace is one option but not required), and (ii) familiarity with prompt engineering basics. The seminar **does not** require participation in Seminar 1.

- At least one (1) MCP integration with a business system the participant selects (e.g., SQL database, document store, CRM, calendar, email).
- An end-to-end integration showing AI consuming real-world business data via MCP.

### Independent stand-alone value

The participant who completes Seminar 3 without prior or subsequent seminars walks away with:
- A working MCP server applicable to any future integration project.
- A business system integration suitable for production use or portfolio demonstration.
- Demonstrable expertise in MCP, currently a scarce skill in the labor market.

### Completion criteria (independent)

- MCP server deployed and tested.
- Business system integration demonstrated end-to-end.
- Architecture documentation submitted.

### No prerequisite required from prior seminars

This seminar assumes (i) participant has access to a development environment, and (ii) participant has a target business system they wish to integrate. The seminar **does not** require participation in Seminars 1 or 2.

## Cross-cutting compliance notes

### Each seminar is independently priced

Each seminar is offered for sale individually at $499. A participant may purchase any one seminar, any subset, or all four. A 25% discount is offered for purchase of all four together ($1,497 bundle).

### Each seminar has independent completion criteria

A participant who completes only one seminar receives a completion record for that seminar. Completion of a seminar does not require enrollment in any other.

### Each seminar produces an independent stand-alone artifact

The artifacts produced in each seminar are usable in the participant's own context independent of the other seminars. They are not "fragments of a course" that only have value when combined.

### Optional integration into a unified architecture

A participant may, at their option, choose to integrate the artifacts across multiple seminars into a single unified architecture, but doing so is **optional and self-directed** and is not required for completion of any individual seminar.

### Faculty and assessment

Each seminar is conducted by Colaberry-affiliated instructors holding the Anthropic Claude Certified Architect — Foundations credential (CCA-F). Each seminar uses independent assessments: scenario questions, reflection, and instructor review.

### Texas Workforce Commission classification

Colaberry submits this seminar series under the Workforce Commission's seminar classification rules, with each individual seminar capped at $499 and meeting the per-seminar criteria of independent learning outcomes, independent artifacts, independent completion, and independent stand-alone value.

## Open questions for counsel review

1. Does the "Lego model" (where a participant who enrolls in all four can integrate artifacts into a single unified architecture) compromise the seminar-independence test if it is optional and self-directed?
2. Are the per-seminar marketing names ("Build Your AI Foundation" / "Create Your AI Team" / "Connect AI To The Real World" / "Design AI That Scales") acceptable, or should we use more formally distinct titles to reinforce independence?
3. The bundle pricing offers a discount when all four are purchased together. Does this compromise the independent-seminar classification, or is bundle pricing for independently-classified seminars acceptable under TWC rules?
4. Should the program use independent instructor pools per seminar, or is a shared instructor pool (Colaberry-affiliated CCA-F-holders) acceptable?
5. Are there per-state registration or filing requirements beyond Texas that we should be aware of, given that the seminars are offered online and may be purchased by Texas residents and non-Texas residents?

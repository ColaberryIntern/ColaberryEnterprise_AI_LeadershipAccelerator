TWC compliance - the seminar split

Texas Workforce Commission rule: anything labeled a "course" triggers heavy audits. "Seminars" capped at $499 each. The research's solution: sell the 12 weeks as 4 independent 3-week "Architect Intensives" at $499 each, bundled at $1,497.

The 4 intensives
  • Intensive 1: Build Your AI Foundation (Wks 1-3) - $499
  • Intensive 2: Create Your AI Team (Wks 4-6) - $499
  • Intensive 3: Connect AI To The Real World (Wks 7-9) - $499
  • Intensive 4: Design AI That Scales (Wks 10-12) - $499
  • Bundle: $1,497 - "Get Seminar 4 Free"
The compliance tension

The Lego model means Intensive 2 builds on Intensive 1. That can read as "one course chopped up" to TWC, which is what we're trying to avoid.

Fix: each intensive must produce a SELF-CONTAINED deliverable with independent learning outcomes, even though they live in the same student's project repo. Counsel review required.

Risk
TWC counsel may push back on the seminar split if the Lego model isn't defensible. Mitigation: explicit per-intensive outcome statements + per-intensive artifact catalog by Jun 13.

Cert & assessment

  • External: Claude Certified Architect - Foundations (CCA-F). ~60 questions, ~120 min, scenario-based, 720/1000 to pass. ~$99 retail, free for first 5,000 Anthropic Partner Network employees.
  • Internal: per-module 10 scenario questions + reflection + AI Mentor review.
  • AI Architect Readiness Score (0-100) across MCP / Claude Code / Architecture / Reliability / Governance.

5. Marketing - viral video is the engine

The marketing strategy from the research is not paid acquisition. It's: students build in public, the platform auto-formats their weekly progress into LinkedIn posts + short video scripts + architecture updates, and the cohort's Architect Expo becomes a recurring marketing event.

The viral content engine
  • Build Log auto-formatter - per student, per week, generates LinkedIn post + short video script + architecture update + demo summary from their GitHub activity + reflection
  • Architect Expo recordings - cohort graduations turn into marketing content for the next cohort
  • Identity branding - "Colaberry AI Architects" (modeled on CrossFitters / Harley Riders / AWS Architects)
  • Public Build Logs - in-platform feed students post to with consent
What's NOT in the plan (yet)
  • Paid acquisition - no CAC target, no Meta/LinkedIn ad budget discussed
  • Affiliate / referral program - not designed
  • Outbound enterprise sales - the "employer-paid" persona needs a B2B motion we don't have
  • SEO content - not part of the launch plan
Decision needed
What's the paid-channel backstop? Viral works AFTER the first cohort. The Founding Cohort has nothing to viral off. We need a launch-month paid budget + 2-3 confirmed testimonials (Anthropic Partner cohort grads) to seed week 1. Define the launch-month marketing spend.
Decision needed
Consent + moderation on auto-posts. The auto-formatter can fire LinkedIn posts in the student's name. They'll need an opt-in toggle + a review queue (someone on Marketing spot-checks before they publish). Otherwise we risk a student finding a weird AI-generated post on their LinkedIn the next morning.

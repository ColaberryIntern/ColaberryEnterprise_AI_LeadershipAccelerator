# TWC Compliance Context

**For anyone whose work touches the marketing claim "4 seminars" or the curriculum — read this before writing copy or building enrollment flow.**

---

## The legal frame (plain English)

Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) regulates career schools. Their classification distinguishes between **a course** (longer-form regulated instruction) and **a seminar** (short, focused, independently-valuable instruction). Seminars carry lighter regulatory burden.

We're selling the AI Systems Architect Accelerator as **four (4) independent stackable Architect Intensives**, each $499, not as a single 12-week course. The bundle ($1,497) is a marketing discount, not a course prerequisite.

For each intensive to qualify as a seminar, it has to demonstrably stand alone — independent outcomes, independent artifacts, independent completion criteria, independent stand-alone value to a participant who enrolls only in that one.

---

## The Lego model tension

Our curriculum is a **Lego model** — one coherent system across 12 weeks, each intensive building on the prior. That can read as "one course chopped up" if we're not careful.

**Resolution:** each intensive produces a **self-contained, demonstrably-valuable deliverable** even if the participant never enrolls in the other three.

| # | Intensive | Self-contained deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Build Your AI Foundation | Architect Workspace + 3 reusable AI Skills + working Business Workflow Assistant |
| 2 | Create Your AI Team | Working multi-agent system (agent definitions, coordination, role-specific behavior) |
| 3 | Connect AI To The Real World | First production MCP server connecting to real business data |
| 4 | Design AI That Scales | Production-readiness package: reliability layer + governance layer + architecture documentation |

A participant who finishes only Intensive 1 walks away with a functioning Architect Workspace they can use on any future AI project — not just a partial credit toward something incomplete.

## How marketing copy must read

Throughout `training.colaberry.com` and any landing page:

- Each intensive has its own landing page (`training.colaberry.com/intensive/build-your-ai-foundation/` etc.)
- Each landing page describes the intensive as a stand-alone purchase. Bundle is offered separately.
- **Don't say:** "Week 4 of the program" or "Prerequisite: Intensive 1." Use language like "Intensive 2: Create Your AI Team — for participants who want to build a multi-agent system from scratch, no prerequisites required."
- **Do say:** Outcome statements + artifacts language from the TWC doc. Reference the standalone value.
- **Avoid:** "Course," "semester," "module 1 / 2 / 3" in marketing copy. "Intensive" is the consistent vocabulary.

CB System — Architecture & Management Overview

One page to understand the autonomous agent that runs across Basecamp: every moving part, how the pieces connect, what data it produces, and where the management blind spot is today.

CB System is the shared engine, not a product. It is the autonomous worker that lives in Basecamp — it reads @-mentions, drafts deliverables, scores and tracks to-dos, and reports to Ali. It is what the advisor's /my-day is built on: /my-day is the product surface (kept in the advisor app); CB System is the engine (managed here, in the enterprise app). It connects the two projects.

The moving parts, by layer

1 · Input — senses Basecamp

  • Inbound dispatcher — polls every 3 min for new @CB mentions across all projects; dedups + routes them.
  • Context walker — 4-layer graph walk (todolist → todo → comments → linked docs/PDFs) to give the brain full context.

2 · Brain — decides & acts

  • CB handler (GPT-4o) — interprets the request, calls tools: reply in Basecamp, email Ali, queue a follow-up, complete a to-do, gov-bid actions.
  • Guardrails — drafts only; no external comms; circuit breaker caps duplicate replies; every call logged.

3 · Proactive work — works ahead

  • AI task runners — nightly: auto-draft first-pass deliverables for upcoming AI-tier to-dos (per project).
  • Autonomous worker — every 15 min: runs #auto-* recipe to-dos (grep / SQL / status), posts results.

4 · Mirror & scoring — the data engine

  • BC sync — every 2 min: mirrors Basecamp to-dos into Postgres (now incremental + fast).
  • Priority engine — scores every to-do 0–100 + category (human-required / waiting / unscored).
  • Automation rules — deterministic actions: flag-for-archive, tag-category, count metrics.

5 · Comms — reports out

  • Digests — morning & evening email: overdue / due / waiting-on-Ali.
  • Reminders & backlog enforcer — targeted nudges; flags backlog when it crosses thresholds.
  • Reports runner — fires scheduled reports from a config table.

6 · Health & quality — watches itself

  • Watchdog — daily: parses tick logs, GREEN/YELLOW/RED, emails Ali.
  • Coverage check / self-improve — verifies no @CB miss; regenerates weak past answers.
  • Dup-cleanup & locks — trashes duplicate replies; single-flight guards prevent overlap.

⚠ The management gap

Today you can see CB System only by its footprints:

✓ It posts comments in Basecamp (visible per-thread)

✓ A daily watchdog email (one status snapshot)

✓ Digests to your inbox

✗ No single screen showing is it healthy, what did it do, across which projects, at what cost, with what errors — from a managing perspective.

That screen is what we build in enterprise.

Proposed: CB System Command (the new enterprise screen)

A single management pane fed entirely by data CB System already produces — no new instrumentation needed. Replaces the retired "Run My Day" port at /admin/cb-system.

PANE 1

System health & status

GREEN/YELLOW/RED at a glance: last sync, dispatcher tick health (24h), error count, token state, sync duration, any tripped circuit breaker.

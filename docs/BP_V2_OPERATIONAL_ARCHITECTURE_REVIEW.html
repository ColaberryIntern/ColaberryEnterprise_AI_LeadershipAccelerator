3Lifecycle states — editorial, not telemetric

Brief §7: states should feel editorial, not telemetric. Six maturity bands chosen so each carries operational meaning, not a number.

StateWhat it communicatesDerivation
FoundationalExists, almost nothing is connectedEmpty bucket fallback (no usable, 0% completion)
EmergingFirst pieces moving; cadence formingcompletion ≥5% OR usable>0
CoordinatedPieces fit together; gaps remaincompletion ≥30% OR usable-ratio ≥30%
OperationalRunning reliably end-to-endcompletion ≥55% AND usable-ratio ≥40%
ScalingMature; expanding into new surfacescompletion ≥75% AND usable-ratio ≥60%
StabilizingLate-stage; refinements onlycompletion ≥90% AND usable-ratio ≥80%
Per-domain narratives change with state. Each (domain × state) pair has its own authored line — 6 states × 7 domains = 42 narrative variants in the classifier. Examples:
  • Intake · Foundational — "The doorway into the system is still being built — intake exists but little flows through it yet."
  • Lead Intelligence · Emerging — "Lead systems are beginning to coordinate across intake and routing; scoring is consistent within a single pass."
  • Marketing · Coordinated — "Marketing handoffs connect to lead intelligence and to execution; cadence runs but observability is uneven."
  • Reporting · Operational — "Reporting has high confidence end-to-end — every domain is represented and the numbers reconcile."

4Operational momentum — alive, not snapshot-based

Brief §4: "Replace static % with operational momentum." A small chip on each domain row carries directional movement vs the last snapshot (per-domain, stored in localStorage:bpDomainMomentum:v1, written on visibilitychange/beforeunload).

Seeded prior snapshot from 2026-05-11 · Lead Intelligence chip reads "↓ regressed -18" in muted red · sits next to the EMERGING lifecycle pill · current state was at 0% (current architecture), prior snapshot was 18%
Momentum label thresholds:
  • ↑ improving — delta ≥ +10
  • ↑ progressing — delta ≥ +3
  • ↑ edging up — delta > 0
  • · stable — |delta| < 1 (chip hidden — calm by default)
  • ↓ stalled — delta < 0 small
  • ↓ slipping — delta ≤ -3
  • ↓ regressed — delta ≤ -10
  • baseline — first-visit / no prior snapshot (chip hidden)
Snapshot semantics (matches the Workspace Presence Sprint pattern):
  • Snapshot is frozen in a useRef at session start; momentum is computed against the frozen values, never the live ones
  • Fresh snapshot is written on visibilitychange to hidden, on beforeunload, and on unmount — represents what the operator saw when they LEFT
  • Per-domain timestamps preserved so future enhancements can show "since yesterday" or "in the last week"
  • Same hook pattern reusable for any future per-key delta surface

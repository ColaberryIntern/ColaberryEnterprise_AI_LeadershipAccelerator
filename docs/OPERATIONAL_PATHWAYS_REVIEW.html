3Dependency accent extended through the hierarchy

6Anti-overwhelm audit — three findings, all deferred

Reviewed the live BPs surface for "mountain of tasks" energy. Three observations surfaced. None were shipped as fixes — each touches operator-decision territory or affects vocabulary that the operator just accepted in the prior sprint. Logging here for the next planning round.

Finding 1 — Inherited-context sentence repeats inside large expanded domains.
  • Observation: A priority domain with 14 BPs (e.g., AI & Intelligence) renders the italic "In AI & Intelligence — supports 2 downstream areas" sentence 14 times in immediate vertical sequence.
  • Operational impact: The sentence was designed to anchor each BP to its operational context. Repeated 14 times in sequence, it may shift from anchor to boilerplate.
  • Proposed refinement: Either (a) suppress per-BP inherited context when the domain has > N BPs and surface a single "section context" line above the BP list, or (b) leave as-is and trust that the operator already filters it visually.
  • Why deferred: The operator confirmed the per-BP inheritance approach explicitly in the prior planning round. Changing it now without operator sign-off would re-litigate that decision. Logged for operator call.
Finding 2 — Pathway-stage chip reads as a classification badge, not a sequence anchor.
  • Observation: The new chip uses the same uppercase muted style as the trust-label badge. They visually read as siblings, not as different kinds of signal.
  • Operational impact: The canonical-flow anchor is present but does not stand out as a navigation aid.
  • Proposed refinement: Consider a subtle differentiator — a prefix glyph (↳ or ▸) or a different background tone — so the chip reads as position in sequence rather than another category. Operator preference call.
  • Why deferred: Aesthetic decision; would benefit from operator review of the chip in production before iterating.
Finding 3 — Leverage block doesn't reference pathway-stage vocabulary.
  • Observation: The "Why this matters" line says "Cory's current priority sits in AI & Intelligence — strengthening it would influence Lead Intelligence and Execution Systems." It doesn't mention that AI & Intelligence sits in the Coordination stage.
  • Operational impact: Minor. The operator can read both sentences. Cross-referencing would create cohesion between the two new vocabularies.
  • Proposed refinement: Optionally enrich whyThisMattersSentence in coryPriorityMatcher.ts to compose pathway-stage into the sentence: "Cory's current priority sits in AI & Intelligence (Coordination stage) — …". Adds light coupling from coryPriorityMatcher to pathwayStage.
  • Why deferred: The cross-utility coupling is small but real. Worth a 5-minute operator decision before adding.

Three bounded additions for first-time operators: ambient first-visit framing cards on Cory Home and System BPs, plus three surgical explanatory clauses in existing canonical sentences. No tutorials, no tours, no modals, no tooltip proliferation. The cards dismiss once and never return.

The hard constraint: no tutorials, tooltips, modals, tours, LMS energy. The threaded needle: a small calm card that explains the surface in 3-4 sentences with a "Got it" dismiss button, plus three single-clause explanations embedded in the existing operational vocabulary. A first-time operator reads one card per surface, dismisses, and the workspace looks exactly as it did before — but they now know what they're looking at.
What this sprint explicitly is NOT: a product tour, a guided "next/next/next" sequence, a modal overlay, a chat-based onboarding bot, a separate lexicon page (operator- declined), or a tooltip system. The maximum tooltip footprint is ONE enriched hover title-attr on the pathway-stage chip.

1The first-time-operator gap

An experienced operator landing on Cory Home or System BPs sees:

  • "Today's one priority" — a card with a domain-specific action title
  • "YOU WERE WORKING ON…" continuation
  • Readiness · Coverage · Health tiles
  • "OPERATIONAL LEVERAGE" + "WHY THIS MATTERS" + structural-resilience sentence
  • Pathway-stage chips on every domain (Entry / Coordination / Execution / Reporting)
  • "CURRENT PRIORITY" badge, downstream parentheticals, inherited-context section headers

An experienced operator reads this fluently. A first-time operator gets sophisticated surface signal without embedded explanation of any of the vocabulary.

The constraint set ruled out every standard onboarding pattern: no tours, no modals, no tooltips everywhere, no LMS, no AI-chat onboarding spam. The only viable shape: ambient, embedded, dismissible.

5Insider-knowledge audit — three deferred findings

Reviewed the live surfaces after Phases A + C. Three terms still assume insider context. None shipped as fixes — each touches operator-decision territory or would require non-trivial new infrastructure. Logging for the next planning round.

Finding 1 — "blockers" and "queue" in the Cory Home header.
  • Observation: The page subtitle reads "1 thing on the queue · 2 blockers" — both terms assume the operator knows what the queue tracks and what "blocker" semantically means.
  • Operational impact: Low. The full text is short and the meaning is roughly inferable from context. But a first-time operator might not immediately tie "queue" to Cory's operational work backlog.
  • Proposed refinement: Adjust subtitle to "1 thing Cory's queued · 2 blockers to resolve" — adds a verb that anchors the noun. Single-line edit, no new component.
  • Why deferred: Operator preference call. The current copy is calm; the proposed copy is slightly more explicit but slightly longer.
Finding 2 — "artifact" in priority titles.
  • Observation: Cory's next_action titles use domain-specific jargon like "Create artifact for: GET /api/courses" — the word "artifact" reads as technical jargon to an outside reader.
  • Operational impact: Medium. The priority card is the most-read element on Cory Home; if the title is opaque, the operator stalls.
  • Proposed refinement: Either (a) backend change to expand the "artifact" terminology in next_action.title, or (b) frontend post-processing to rewrite the title. Both are larger than a one-line fix.
  • Why deferred: Crosses into the backend's next-action generation logic. Out of scope for an onboarding-comprehension sprint.
Finding 3 — "CORY · CONTEXTUAL TO HOME" whisper in the context bar.
  • Observation: The persistent context bar carries an italic whisper labeled "CORY · CONTEXTUAL TO HOME" with a tiny "(click for full context)" affordance. The first-visit framing card now explains what Cory is, but the whisper itself doesn't reference back to that explanation.
  • Operational impact: Low. The framing card is sufficient for orientation; the whisper is a tertiary signal.
  • Proposed refinement: No change. The framing card's explanation carries through. If post-launch operator feedback says the whisper is opaque, revisit then.
  • Why deferred: No clear refinement needed — leaving as-is.

7Overall

First-time operators now get an embedded ambient explanation on each of the two main entry surfaces, plus three surgical explanatory clauses inside the canonical operational vocabulary. Sprint-specific learning: the timing-based first-visit detection was tried, deployed, and didn't work in production — React strict- mode + state-poll lifecycle defeated the useRef freeze. The simpler "show until dismissed" gate is robust and arguably better UX (existing operators get the same one-time courtesy as new operators).

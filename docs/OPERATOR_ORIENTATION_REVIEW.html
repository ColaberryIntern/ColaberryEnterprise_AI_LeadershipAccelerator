Operator Orientation + System Impact — Sprint Review

Shipped 2026-05-15 to enterprise.colaberry.ai (commit 7c41fac). The previous sprint taught the system to communicate architecture, causality, and operational pressure. This one teaches it to communicate the operator's place inside the system — where their attention is, what their work influences downstream, and what's moved while they were shaping it.

Before: "Here is a system you are operating."  →  After: "Here is where you are shaping the system." Nothing in this sprint scores the operator, ranks them, predicts their behavior, or tracks activity. Every signal is a reflection of something the operator themselves produced by clicking — no analytics, no AI inference, no tracking infrastructure.
What this sprint explicitly is NOT: operator scoring, productivity analytics, user rankings, activity feeds, behavior tracking, AI personalization, gamification, streaks, points, congratulation, rewards, or PM-tool energy. The calm-language guardrails live in a single file (operatorOrientationLanguage.ts) and are asserted by unit tests.

After the operator engages Lead Intelligence on the System BPs surface (click a flow-strip stop, expand a row, or click a relationship chip), Cory Home reflects that focus back to them on their next visit. 5Honest impact framing — temporal, never causal

The impact line is the most psychologically loaded sentence on Cory Home. It would be very easy to write something like "Your work strengthened readiness by 4 points" — but that's a claim of causation we cannot honestly make. The operator was in this domain while readiness moved. The work and the movement are temporally correlated; we don't know they are causally connected.

Forbidden framingShipped framing
"Your work strengthened readiness by 4 points.""While you were shaping Lead Intelligence, readiness strengthened."
"Thanks to your effort in Intake, coverage expanded.""While you were shaping Intake, coverage expanded."
"You cleared 3 queue items.""While you were shaping Lead Intelligence, the queue got lighter."
Claimed causationTemporal correlation
How this is enforced:
  • All language lives in one file: operatorOrientationLanguage.ts.
  • Unit test asserts the impact sentence never contains "because", "caused", "thanks to", "resulted in".
  • Unit test asserts no exclamation marks and no congratulatory vocabulary ("great", "nice", "awesome", "congrat", "well done").
  • Every language builder returns null when there is nothing meaningful to say — surfaces render nothing rather than fall back to filler.

7Overall verdict

The operator can now see, on Cory Home, where they're shaping the system, what their work flows into downstream, and an honest note on motion while they were there — derived purely from continuity signals they themselves produced.
